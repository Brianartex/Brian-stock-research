The Core Thesis: Two Separate Bets, One Document

These are two distinct investment frameworks in one basket:

💻 Part 1 — Battered Tech

Beaten-down SaaS and software stocks that sold off from ATHs. The thesis: at current valuations, growth rates don't need to re-accelerate dramatically for these to work — they're pricing in a lot of bad news. The risk: if growth keeps decelerating, multiples compress further. These need a catalyst — a new AI product, a competitive win, a re-acceleration quarter — to re-rate.

⚡ Part 2 — Sandisk-Type Plays

The Sandisk DNA: oversupplied or neglected by the market + secular demand catalyst the street hasn't fully priced + a specific capex or partnership announcement that becomes the trigger. These are mid-cap infrastructure and hardware names that are structurally levered to AI datacenter buildout. Many were crushed in early 2026 and are now at interesting entry points.

🐂 Bull Case

The supply constraint narrative is the gift. Arista just raised 2026 guidance with AI networking revenue going from $1.5B to $2.75B (+83%). The stock got crushed on supply chain concerns — specifically, component shortages are constraining how fast Arista can fulfill orders. This is a temporary problem, not a demand problem. The AI datacenter network is the new switching layer. AI compute clusters need high-speed, low-latency network fabrics to move training data between GPUs. Arista's 400G/800G Ethernet switches are the backbone of these AI networks. As hyperscalers build out AI campuses (Microsoft's $80B, Google's $75B, Meta's $60B+ capex), Arista is the picked supplier for the network layer.

Valuation is fair, not expensive. At ~$172, ANET is trading at roughly 30-35x forward earnings. For a company growing AI networking revenue at 80%+ with a $2.75B AI networking backlog, that's reasonable — not cheap, but not bubble territory.

🐻 Bear Case

Supply constraints could persist longer than expected. If Broadcom or Marvell can't supply the custom ASICs that Arista's next-gen switches depend on, the supply constraint becomes a demand destruction story. The risk is that customers go to Cisco or Juniper while waiting, and Arista loses design wins.

The hyperscaler capex cycle could pause. If AI training ROI disappoints (models don't deliver productivity gains fast enough), hyperscalers could cut capex. Arista is the most levered pure-play to hyperscaler AI networking spend — a capex pullback hits ANET harder than most.

🎯 My Call

BUY. This is the closest thing to the 2025 Sandisk setup in the market today. A company raised guidance, crushed estimates, and got crushed on the stock because the market focused on the wrong thing (supply) instead of the right thing (demand). AI networking is a secular buildout, not a one-quarter beat. ANET is the highest conviction name in this section.

Entry: $160-170 is an attractive entry. Target: $220+ if AI networking revenue re-accelerates in Q2 earnings. Trigger: Any Q1 earnings confirmation that supply constraint is resolving. Risk: If supply constraints persist into Q2 and guidance is lowered, the stock could fall to $140 — which would be an even better entry.

Verdict: BUY. Top conviction pick in the Sandisk section. Beat/raise + supply constraint overreaction + AI networking secular bull = high probability of re-rating. Size up on any Q1 earnings confirmation of supply recovery.

🧠 Synthesis — The Three Themes Driving This Basket

Across 23 stocks, three structural themes emerge:

1. The Silicon-Photonics Convergence. AI datacenters are hitting a copper wall. Electrical interconnects can't keep up with GPU bandwidth demands. The solution — co-packaged optics and electro-photonics — is creating an entirely new semiconductor supply chain. MRVL (Peng photonics integration), HIMX (CPO leader), and ANET (optical switching) are positioned at different layers of this stack. This is not a 2026 trade — it's a 2027-2030 structural shift that the market has barely begun to price.

2. Power Management as the Bottleneck. Every NVIDIA Blackwell GPU draws 1,200W. A rack of 72 draws 86kW — enough to power a small office building. The companies solving power delivery, conversion, and cooling are the picks-and-shovels of the AI buildout. AOSL (server power semis, +40% seq), ETN (grid infrastructure), VST (power generation), and AMKR (advanced packaging) are all levered to the same thesis: AI doesn't work without reliable power.

3. The Inference- Everywhere Thesis. Training gets the headlines, but inference is where the installed base lives. As AI moves from the training cluster to every device and application, CPU efficiency matters as much as GPU throughput. ARM (CPU architecture for AGI), DDOG (observability for AI stacks), and QCOM (edge inference) benefit from the broadening of AI beyond the datacenter. This is the "second wave" that follows the GPU gold rush.
