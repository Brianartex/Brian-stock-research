Keeper Decision Engine

The math, refreshed. ADP → projected auction value (superflex QB ×1.3), minus keeper cost = surplus. One recommendation, with the alternative shown honestly.

✅ Recommended — Puka + Dart

Puka Nacua ($21) + Jaxson Dart ($8) = $29 locked · $171 to auction
Puka is a locked-in WR1 at $21 — the single best value on the roster (+$29 surplus). Dart at $8 is a starting superflex QB — in a format where any starting QB is worth $15+, that's league-winning value (+$10 surplus). Keeping a QB frees ~$50 to attack RBs/WRs at auction instead of overpaying for one. Dart is year-2 (not a rookie) and ran for 9 TDs in 14 starts — the Konami-code floor is real.

SOURCED: FantasyPros ECR (Puka WR4, Jul 11 2026); Josh Norris QB tiers (Dart QB10, 89th overall); Reddit r/fantasyfootball (Dart year-2, 400 rushing yds).

⚡ Alternative — Puka + Kyren

Puka Nacua ($21) + Kyren Williams ($21) = $42 locked · $158 to auction
If you'd rather secure an RB1, Kyren at $21 is a high-end RB2 with RB1 upside (+$11 surplus). The catch: Blake Corum ate into his workload late in 2025 (Kyren down to 68% snaps by Week 13), and the Rams added Davante Adams, thinning Kyren's target share. You'd enter the auction needing a QB1 — expensive in superflex. Keep Dart as the auction QB2 instead.

SOURCED: Footballguys snap counts; Rams Wire; keeper-value methodology (ADP 22 → $32 model value).
🔑 Verdict: Puka + Dart is the higher-ceiling play — it banks the league's best WR value AND a starting QB for $8, then lets you build the rest at auction. Puka + Kyren is safer at RB but leaves you QB-hungry. Either way, do not keep Burrow, Amon-Ra, or CMC — their keeper cost equals full auction price (zero surplus).
