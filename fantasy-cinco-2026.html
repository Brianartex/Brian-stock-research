Keeper Strategy — How to Decide
Keep If...
  • Player is projected top-5 at their position and keeper cost is below market ADP
  • Player was drafted for $1-5 and is now a starter (free money)
  • You have a top-3 QB in superflex — the positional scarcity is real
  • Rookie RBs who broke out (Jeanty $53 keep, Hampton $30 keep)
Don't Keep If...
  • Keeper cost exceeds projected auction value (you're overpaying)
  • Player is aging out (McCaffrey at $53 is risky at his age/injury history)
  • You can get similar production for less in the draft
  • You're keeping a player just because you like them — be ruthless

Round-by-round targets for a superflex auction, accounting for keeper depletion. Based on 2025 Cinco League data and early 2026 ADP.

Draft Strategy — Superflex Auction

With Puka ($21) and Kyren ($21) kept, you have $158 for 13 roster spots. Note: Kyren workload trended down to ~68% snaps late in 2025 with Corum taking more work. He is a high-end RB2, not a locked-in RB1. Factor this into your draft plan. That is roughly $12/player average. Your priorities:

1. Spend big on one elite QB ($45-55). In superflex, you need a top-5 QB. Josh Allen, Lamar Jackson, and Jalen Hurts will all go for $48-55. Budget $50 for your QB1 — do not skimp here.
2. Fill out WR2/WR3 with mid-tier targets ($10-20 each). You already have Puka as WR1. Target two more WRs in the $10-20 range — players like DJ Moore, DeVonta Smith, or sophomore breakouts.
3. Draft a second QB late ($5-10). A QB2 like Geno Smith, Baker Mayfield, or a rookie like Jaxson Dart gives you superflex flexibility without breaking the bank.
4. Dollar-menu RBs and TEs ($1-5 each). Fill your bench with high-upside backups, rookie stashes, and a cheap TE. You do not need to spend big at TE in a non-TE-premium league.
