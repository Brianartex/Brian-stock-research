bull_case

The AI Agent wave is DocuSign's next act. AI agents need to execute agreements. DocuSign has 1.5B+ agreements — the largest structured agreement dataset.

Valuation is pricing in collapse. ~6x forward EV/revenue for a company still growing. Risk/reward asymmetrically favors buyers.

bear_case

AI could disintermediate DocuSign entirely. If Microsoft, Google, or OpenAI build agreement execution into their platforms, DocuSign's moat shrinks.

The turnaround isn't confirmed. One quarter of stabilization doesn't make a trend. Billings growth remains weak.

Verdict HOLD

The AI tailwind is there but not yet in the numbers. At 6x revenue the market is pricing this like it's dying — it isn't. I'd open a starter at $48 and wait for a re-acceleration quarter before sizing up. What would change my mind: Q2 FY2027 showing AI Agent Studio contributing more than 5% of revenue. Or a competitor launching something that makes DocuSign's agreement moat look thin.

bull_case

AI's actual bottleneck isn't chips — it's power and cooling. Every GPU needs thermal management, backup power, and electrical infrastructure. Vertiv sells all three. They're the picks-and-shovels play on the entire AI buildout, not just one vendor's product cycle.

This is what Eaton was to electrification in the 20th century. The data center buildout is a decade-long capex cycle. Vertiv's order book keeps growing and their guidance keeps moving up. 40% EPS growth at 40x forward isn't cheap, but compounders at this growth rate rarely are.

bear_case

You're paying up for growth that might not come. At 40x forward earnings, any miss gets punished hard. Hyperscaler capex is lumpy — if Microsoft or Google pause a buildout cycle, orders dry up. The stock has already run 340% from its 2024 lows.

Competition isn't standing still. Eaton, Schneider Electric, and ABB all want this market. Vertiv's moat is real but it's not unbreachable. If a competitor undercuts on price during the next hyperscaler RFP cycle, margins compress.

Verdict BUY

Best pure-play AI infrastructure compounder I've found. The valuation makes me pause — you're not getting a bargain at 40x. But quality at a fair price beats junk at a discount. Open a half position here, add the rest if it pulls back to the 200-day. What kills the thesis: A hyperscaler capex pause or a miss on Q3 guidance. Watching — Not Yet Ready to Recommend

STX — Seagate. The HDD cycle play looks interesting but Seagate is getting squeezed on both sides: NAND/flash keeps eating HDD market share from above, and the hyperscaler storage buildout hasn't materialized in their numbers yet. I want to see a quarter where nearline HDD revenue actually accelerates before putting this back in the basket.
MU — Micron (for now). The HBM memory thesis is one of the best in semis — 7x forward P/E for a company that should print cash in an AI supercycle is absurd. But memory cycles are vicious and this stock can halve before the thesis plays out. I'm waiting for a pullback below $80 or confirmation that HBM3E volume is ramping ahead of schedule. Currently in the Tier 1 basket at a 7x multiple — will revisit.
