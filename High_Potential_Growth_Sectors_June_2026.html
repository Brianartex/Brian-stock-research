The Geopolitical Inflection Point — May 2026

The U.S.-Iran conflict that began in February 2026 has fundamentally restructured global commodity supply chains in ways that will persist for years, not months. The Strait of Hormuz — through which 20-25% of global oil, 27-30% of global helium, and ~30% of global seaborne fertilizer trade passes — is now a active war zone. Qatar's Ras Laffan helium plant is offline. Iranian fertilizer and methanol exports have effectively ceased. The result is the largest commodity supply shock since the 1970s OPEC embargo.

But this is not a simple "buy oil stocks" narrative. The shock is asymmetric, the winners are specific, and the bear cases are substantive. This report applies a Council of Expert Opinions to each sector — separating genuine structural winners from hype, and identifying where the bull case is already priced in.
