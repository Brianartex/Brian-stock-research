Macro Framework

The $1 Trillion Question — And Why It Favors Our Picks

As [1] laid out: total projected EBITDA across AI baskets exceeds total capex by $1 trillion. Two things must be true: (1) AI drains $1T of profit from elsewhere in the economy — likely SaaS and white-collar labor — and (2) some EBITDA estimates are wildly optimistic. Our basket is positioned for both. We avoid the EBITDA donors (SaaS) and the most optimistic baskets. We own the infrastructure layer where revenue is already real — memory, networking, power infrastructure, and the physical buildout. These are the picks-and-shovels of the AI capex cycle, and they get paid regardless of which AI model wins.

The semiconductor talent shortage — software engineers earn 3x what fab engineers make — means memory and storage supply will be structurally constrained for 2-3 years. Pricing power sits with MU, SNDK, and the memory complex. This is not a demand story. It is a supply story.
[2] — Semiconductor Talent Shortage Thesis

The stocks we're betting on share a common DNA: structural demand that isn't going away + a specific catalyst the market hasn't fully priced + financials that work even if the catalyst takes longer than expected. They are infrastructure beneficiaries, not EBITDA donors. The AI buildout happens with or without the models delivering — and that is the margin of safety.

Investment Methodology

The SNDK Playbook — Finding the Next Asymmetric Opportunity

SanDisk was under $30 a year ago. It is now $1,562 — a 5,100% return. The playbook was simple: find a company that was essential to the AI supply chain, losing money in the short term, but supplying a product that hyperscalers could not build without. The market priced SNDK like it was going bankrupt. The hyperscalers kept ordering. The stock re-rated.

This basket applies the same playbook to the current market:

What We Look For
  • Essential to the AI infrastructure supply chain — hyperscalers cannot build without it
  • Trading at a valuation that prices in failure, not the optionality of success
  • A specific, identifiable catalyst within 12-18 months that changes the financial math
  • Wall Street targets that do not include the catalyst — creating asymmetric upside when estimates get revised
  • Financials that work even if the catalyst takes longer than expected — margin of safety
What We Avoid
  • Companies with no revenue floor — a thesis without financials is a lottery ticket
  • Stocks that have already run past all analyst targets — the catalyst is priced in
  • Companies where the thesis depends on a single binary event with no fallback
  • SaaS companies being disrupted by AI — the EBITDA donors, not the beneficiaries
  • Anything with fewer than 2 analysts covering and negative triple-digit margins

Independent research and analysis supports this framework [1] [2]. The semiconductor talent shortage creates multi-year supply constraints [2]. AI revenue growth at companies like Anthropic validates the capex cycle is real [6]. Optical and photonics bottlenecks are the next frontier [3] [5]. This basket is the result of applying the playbook systematically, not following any single source.

Risk Management

How To Lose $2,000 — And How Not To

This basket is not diversified in the traditional sense. Most of the picks are levered to the same macro thesis: AI infrastructure buildout continues, hyperscaler capex keeps flowing, and the semiconductor supply constraint persists. If that thesis breaks — if hyperscalers pause spending, if memory cycle turns, if AI hype fades — most of these stocks go down together. That is the bet. You are either right about the AI capex cycle or you are wrong. Being half-right doesn't help.

What To Do If It Works
  • Take partial profits when a stock doubles. $300 FLNC → $600: sell $300, let the rest ride.
  • Reinvest gains into the laggards or new catalysts.
  • Don't fall in love with a stock just because it went up. Re-evaluate the thesis quarterly.
  • If the macro thesis holds but a specific stock underperforms, cut it and redeploy.
What To Do If It Doesn't
  • $2,000 is the max loss. Do not add to losing positions hoping they turn around.
  • If the macro thesis breaks (capex pause, memory cycle turns), cut everything.
  • If a specific stock's catalyst fails (FLNC MSAs expire, SMCI audit fails), cut that stock regardless of what others are doing.
  • The worst outcome: averaging down on a broken thesis. Don't.
