Reading Each Stock Card
Price Action Bar
6-month, 90-day, 30-day, and YTD returns. Green = positive, red = negative. Look for the 30D vs 90D relationship — if 30D is green but 90D is red, a reversal may be forming. Reversal badges confirm when the short-term trend has flipped.
Sparkline
6-month price history in miniature. Shows the shape of the move — smooth climb, V-reversal, or grinding decline. A stock in a steady uptrend with small pullbacks is healthier than one that spikes and crashes.
Bull / Bear Cases
What needs to go right. What could go wrong. Both are essential — never read one without the other. The most dangerous investments are the ones where you can only see the bull case.
Verdict
BUY, HOLD, WATCH, or SKIP with specific reasoning and the catalyst to watch for. A BUY without a catalyst is just a hope. A HOLD with a clear trigger tells you exactly when to upgrade.
Understanding the Financial Metrics
Revenue (Top Line)
Total sales over the past fiscal year. Look at the YoY growth rate — is revenue accelerating, decelerating, or flat? For SaaS companies, 15%+ is healthy, 25%+ is strong. Below 10% signals a maturing business. The absolute number tells you the company scale.
Free Cash Flow (FCF)
Cash the business generates after paying for operations and capital expenditures. This is real money — not accounting profit. A company with high FCF can buy back stock, make acquisitions, or weather downturns. FCF margin (FCF / Revenue) above 15% is excellent. Negative FCF means the company is burning cash — acceptable for hypergrowth, dangerous for a mature business.
Gross Margin
Revenue minus cost of goods sold, as a percentage. Shows pricing power and business model quality. 70%+ = exceptional (software companies with little marginal cost). 50-70% = strong (differentiated hardware, platforms). 30-50% = average (manufacturing, services). Below 30% = commoditized (low pricing power). A declining gross margin is a red flag — it means competition is squeezing you.
Net Cash
Cash and equivalents minus total debt. A positive number means the company has more cash than debt — it can survive downturns, buy back stock, or make acquisitions. A negative number means net debt — the company owes more than it has. Not automatically bad (debt can fuel growth), but check whether the debt load is manageable relative to FCF.
Operating Margin (GAAP)
Operating income divided by revenue. This is profitability after all operating costs — the real bottom line before interest and taxes. 20%+ = excellent (highly profitable). 10-20% = solid. 0-10% = thin margins (vulnerable to cost shocks). Negative = unprofitable (spending more than it earns — acceptable for growth companies, concerning for mature ones). GAAP means it includes stock-based compensation — a more honest measure than "adjusted" margins.
Forward P/E in Context
Price divided by expected next-12-months earnings. This is context-dependent: Under 10x = deeply cheap (market expects decline — high risk, high reward). 10-20x = reasonable for most companies. 20-40x = growth premium (must deliver above-average growth). 40x+ = expensive (pricing in perfection — any miss gets punished hard). The key question: does the growth rate justify the multiple? A company growing 50% at 50x P/E is arguably cheap. A company growing 3% at 35x P/E is a value trap.
The Core Thesis — Three Investment Frameworks, One Document

This basket tracks three distinct categories of opportunity. They share a common thread — asymmetric risk/reward at valuations that price in collapse — but each requires a different approach.

SaaS Survivors

Beaten-down software stocks that sold off 50-90% from ATHs. The market is pricing these like they're going to zero. Most won't. At 6-10x forward revenue with 75%+ gross margins, they don't need to re-accelerate dramatically — they just need to not die. The AI agent thesis adds a genuine platform expansion layer to names like DOCU, SNOW, and DDOG.

AI Infrastructure

Hardware, networking, power, and data center plays riding the AI capex wave. This is the Sandisk DNA: oversupplied or neglected by the market + secular demand the street hasn't priced + a specific catalyst that becomes the trigger. Names like ANET, GLW, and STX are printing cash while the market fixates on GPU names. Cyclical risk is real — position size accordingly.

Top 7 Picks — May 9, 2026

Ranked by asymmetric upside, analyst conviction, and catalyst clarity. Updated with fresh research across all doc picks.

Synthesis — The Three Currents Driving These Picks

1. The AI Infrastructure Buildout Is Real — And the Revenue Proves It. Anthropic went from $9B to $45B ARR in 4 months. Hyperscaler capex is $250B+. Memory (MU, SNDK, STX), networking (ANET), and power infrastructure (VST, ETN) get paid regardless of which AI model wins. These are picks-and-shovels, not lottery tickets.

2. The Semiconductor Talent Shortage Creates Multi-Year Pricing Power. Software engineers earn 3x what fab engineers make. It takes 3-4 years to train a competent semiconductor engineer. Supply will be structurally constrained through 2028. Companies that control memory and storage supply (MU, SNDK) have pricing power they haven't had in decades. This is not a demand story — it is a supply story.

3. Wall Street Targets Are Floors, Not Ceilings — For the Stocks With Real Catalysts. FLNC's targets exclude $5.6B in backlog. AAOI has only 5 analysts — no one is modeling the revenue doubling. SMCI at 11x P/E prices in accounting risk, not the xAI deal. The alpha is in the stocks where analyst models haven't caught up to the catalysts.

#1
ANET — Arista Networks
BUY

Best risk/reward in the entire doc. 26 analysts, Strong Buy (1.31 — highest conviction). Mean target $189 (33% upside from $141). 42.7% operating margins, $4.36B FCF, 35% revenue growth. Stock is BELOW targets with elite margins. Consolidating between 50d/200d MAs — healthy basing pattern. The networking backbone for every AI cluster. Catalyst: Steady earnings beats — no drama, just execution.

#2
MU — Micron Technology
BUY

196% revenue growth at 7x forward P/E. 42 analysts, Strong Buy (1.45). The HBM memory supercycle + semiconductor talent shortage means pricing power for years. Yes, the stock is 34% above the mean target ($556) — but at 7x P/E, the target is wrong, not the stock price. Up 194% in 6 months. Do not chase — buy on pullback to $550-650. Catalyst: Q2 2026 HBM pricing power confirmation. HBM4 progress.

#3
VST — Vistra Corp
BUY

55% upside to analyst targets — second best in the basket. 18 analysts, Strong Buy (1.40). 43% revenue growth, 26.6% operating margins. Owns nuclear (Comanche Peak 2.3 GW) + 39 GW of gas/coal/solar. AI data centers need 24/7 baseload power. Nuclear earns premium prices when gas is expensive — and gas IS expensive. Catalyst: Any hyperscaler PPA announcement with Vistra nuclear assets.

#4
STX — Seagate Technology
BUY

22 analysts, Strong Buy (1.50). 44% revenue growth, 35.7% operating margins, $1.61B FCF. Up 166% in 6 months. The HDD cycle is working and AI storage buildout provides multi-year demand. At 35.7% margins, this is not a commodity business. Catalyst: Nearline HDD revenue acceleration from hyperscaler storage buildout.

#5
MRVL — Marvell Technology
BUY

39 analysts, Strong Buy (1.49). Photonics is the differentiator — if Penguin electro-optics integration succeeds, MRVL has a multi-year lead in solving the critical AI datacenter bottleneck. Custom ASIC at $8.2B scale is a compounding machine. Stock is 31% above targets — photonics thesis is partially priced in. Catalyst: First photonics-enabled product announcement with named hyperscaler customer.

#6
ARM — Arm Holdings
BUY

36 analysts, Buy (1.90). The CPU-for-AI thesis is underappreciated — inference at scale needs efficient CPU architecture, not just GPUs. Arm v9 royalty ramp provides multi-year visibility. Asset-light model with 65%+ gross margins. 29.6% operating margins. Catalyst: Next earnings confirming AI CPU royalty acceleration.

#7
DDOG — Datadog
HOLD

47 analysts, Strong Buy (1.42). 32% revenue growth, 80% gross margins, $937M FCF. The most widely covered stock in the basket. Datadog is the observability layer every AI deployment needs. The moat widens as stacks get more complex. Premium valuation is the only concern. Catalyst: AI-specific product revenue disclosure showing inflection.
