Understanding the Financial Snapshot
Price
The current share price. By itself, price tells you nothing — a $500 stock can be cheaper than a $5 stock. Always look at price in context of earnings (P/E) and market cap.
YTD Return
Year-to-date percentage change. Shows momentum — is the market already pricing in the thesis, or is it still under the radar? A stock up 100%+ YTD means the thesis is being discovered. A stock flat or down means the market has not yet priced in the opportunity — but check why.
Market Cap
Total value of the company (share price x shares outstanding). Under $10B = small cap (higher growth potential, higher volatility). $10-100B = mid cap (established but still room to run). $100B+ = large/mega cap (more stable, slower moving). Small caps can double faster but can also halve. Size your position accordingly.
Forward P/E
Price divided by expected earnings over the next 12 months. This is the single most important valuation metric. Under 15x = cheap (market is pessimistic — could be a bargain or a value trap). 15-25x = fairly valued for a growing company. 25-40x = growth premium (market expects strong future growth — must deliver). 40x+ = expensive (pricing in near-perfect execution — any miss gets punished). Negative = unprofitable (pre-revenue or investing for growth — speculative). The key question: does the growth rate justify the multiple? A company growing 40% at 40x P/E is fairly priced. A company growing 5% at 40x P/E is dangerously expensive.
The Core Thesis — Second-Order Effects of the Iran War

The Iran war most investable effects are not the obvious ones. Defense stocks spiked on Day 1 — that trade is over. What is underappreciated: the Strait of Hormuz closure has removed 21 million barrels per day of oil from global markets, and with it, the entire Gulf petrochemical, fertilizer, and helium supply chain. These sectors represent structural supply shortages that take years to resolve — not temporary price spikes.

Supply Destruction

Oil, fertilizer precursors, petrochemical feedstocks, and helium — all transit the Strait of Hormuz. With the strait closed, these commodities face physical supply removal. New supply sources take 2-5 years.

Demand Shift

When Gulf supply disappears, demand reroutes. US natural gas liquids, Brazilian fertilizer, Canadian helium, and Australian nuclear fuel become the alternatives. Companies controlling these supply chains gain pricing power.

AI Power Nexus

AI data centers need massive, reliable power. The same instability that disrupts oil also accelerates investment in nuclear, battery storage, and grid infrastructure — a new intersection of energy security and AI infrastructure demand.

Core Thesis

Combined with Russia's helium export restrictions, this leaves global semiconductor manufacturers with a critical shortage of a non-substitutable resource.
